What is the Stock Market?

The stock market is a platform where investors can buy and sell shares of publicly traded companies. It plays a crucial role in the economy by allowing companies to raise capital to fund operations and growth, while providing investors with opportunities to earn returns on their investments.

Market Participants
  1. Retail Investors

    Individual investors who buy and sell securities for their personal accounts. They make up a significant portion of market participants and contribute to market liquidity.

  2. Institutional Investors

    Entities such as mutual funds, pension funds, insurance companies, and banks that invest large sums of money in the market. They often have a significant influence on market trends due to the size of their investments.

  3. Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs)

    Non-Indian entities that invest in the Indian stock market. Their participation can bring substantial capital into the market and influence stock prices.

  4. Domestic Institutional Investors (DIIs)

    Indian entities such as domestic mutual funds and insurance companies that invest in the local markets.

  5. Market Makers

    Firms or individuals that provide liquidity to the market by buying and selling securities at publicly quoted prices.

  6. Regulators

    Bodies like the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) that oversee the functioning of the stock market to ensure fairness, transparency, and investor protection.
