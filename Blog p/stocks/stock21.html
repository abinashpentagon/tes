Break of Structure (BoS)

A Break of Structure (BoS) occurs when the price action surpasses a significant support or resistance level that was previously acting as a key barrier. This break signifies a potential change in the market’s structure, suggesting that the previous trend or consolidation phase may be coming to an end.

Image:

Key Concepts of Break of Structure
Market Structure

  • Higher Highs and Higher Lows (Uptrend): In an uptrend, the market makes successive higher highs and higher lows. A break of this structure occurs when the price fails to create a new higher high or higher low.

  • Lower Lows and Lower Highs (Downtrend): In a downtrend, the market makes successive lower lows and lower highs. A break of this structure occurs when the price fails to create a new lower low or lower high.

Types of Breaks

  • Bullish Break of Structure: Occurs when the price breaks above a previous high or resistance level, signaling a potential shift from a bearish to a bullish trend.

  • Bearish Break of Structure: Occurs when the price breaks below a previous low or support level, signaling a potential shift from a bullish to a bearish trend.

Significance of BoS

  • Trend Reversal: A break of structure often indicates a potential reversal in the trend. For example, if the market has been in an uptrend and breaks below a previous low, it may signal a possible trend reversal to the downside.

  • Trend Continuation: In some cases, a break of structure can confirm the continuation of the prevailing trend. For instance, if the market breaks above a resistance level during an uptrend, it might suggest that the uptrend will continue.

Confirmation

  • Volume: A BoS is more reliable if it is accompanied by increased trading volume. High volume indicates strong interest and commitment from traders and institutions.

  • Retest: Often, after a BoS, the price may retest the broken level. For example, after breaking a support level, the price might return to that level, which now acts as resistance.

How to Identify Break of Structure
Chart Analysis

  • Identify Key Levels: Look for significant support and resistance levels on the chart. These are the levels where previous breaks of structure occurred.

  • Observe Price Action: Monitor how the price behaves around these key levels. A break is confirmed when the price moves significantly beyond these levels.

Use Technical Indicators

  • Moving Averages: Moving averages can help identify the trend and potential breaks in structure. Crossovers of moving averages may signal a break.

  • Trendlines: Drawing trendlines on the chart can help visualize breaks of structure. A break of the trendline may indicate a change in market structure.

Volume Analysis

  • Volume Spikes: Look for volume spikes accompanying the break. Increased volume confirms the strength of the break and the potential change in market structure.

Patterns

  • Chart Patterns: Certain chart patterns, such as head and shoulders or double tops/bottoms, can signify a break of structure.

Examples of Break of Structure
Bullish Break of Structure

  • Scenario: The market is in a downtrend with successive lower lows. The price then breaks above a significant previous high.

  • Interpretation: This break might indicate a shift in trend from bearish to bullish, suggesting potential buying opportunities.

Bearish Break of Structure

  • Scenario: The market is in an uptrend with successive higher highs. The price then breaks below a significant previous low.

  • Interpretation: This break might indicate a shift in trend from bullish to bearish, suggesting potential selling opportunities.

Practical Application

  • Confirm with Other Indicators: Use other technical indicators and analysis methods to confirm the break of structure and assess its significance.

  • Set Entry and Exit Points: Based on the break of structure, set entry points for trades and determine stop-loss and take-profit levels.

  • Monitor for Retests: Watch for retests of the broken levels to confirm the strength and validity of the break.

  • Adjust Strategies: Be prepared to adjust trading strategies based on the new market structure and emerging trends.

Conclusion

A Break of Structure (BoS) is a valuable concept in technical analysis for identifying potential shifts in market trends. By understanding and identifying these breaks, traders can make informed decisions about entering or exiting trades, adjusting strategies, and managing risk. Combining BoS analysis with other technical tools and indicators can enhance trading decisions and improve overall strategy effectiveness.
