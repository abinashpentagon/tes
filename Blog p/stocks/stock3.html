Introduction to Stock Market

By Admin | Posted on 27 JULY 2024 | 07 min read

Stock/Share

  1. Definition: A stock (or share) represents a unit of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a shareholder and own a small part of that company.

  2. Example: If you buy 100 shares of Tata Motors, you own a portion of Tata Motors.

Initial Public Offering (IPO)

  1. Definition: An IPO is the process through which a private company offers its shares to the public for the first time. It allows the company to raise capital from public Investors

  2. Example: When Zomato went public in 2021, it issued shares through an IPO.

Bull Market

  1. Definition: A bull market is a period during which stock prices are rising or are expected to rise. It is characterized by investor confidence and optimism.

  2. Example: The stock market experienced a bull run from 2016 to 2019, with major indices reaching record highs.

Bear Market

  1. Definition: A bear market is a period during which stock prices are falling or are expected to fall. It is characterized by investor pessimism and fear.

  2. Example: The global financial crisis of 2008 led to a prolonged bear market.

Dividend

  1. Definition: A dividend is a portion of a company's earnings distributed to shareholders. Dividends can be issued in cash or additional shares.

  2. Example: Reliance Industries declared a dividend of ₹7 per share in a recent financial year.

Broker

  1. Definition: A broker is a person or firm that executes buy and sell orders on behalf of investors. Brokers charge a fee or commission for their services.

  2. Example: Zerodha and ICICI Direct are popular brokerage firms in India.

Portfolio

  1. Definition: A portfolio is a collection of investments owned by an individual or institution. It can include stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and other assets.

  2. Example: An investor’s portfolio might include shares of Infosys, bonds issued by the government, and units of a mutual fund.

Market Capitalization (Market Cap)

  1. Definition:Market capitalization is the total market value of a company’s outstanding shares. It is calculated as the share price multiplied by the number of shares outstanding.

  2. Example: If TCS has 1 billion shares outstanding and the share price is ₹3,000, its market cap is ₹3 trillion.

Liquidity

  1. Definition: Liquidity refers to the ease with which an asset can be bought or sold in the market without affecting its price. Highly liquid assets can be quickly converted into cash.

  2. Example: Large-cap stocks like HDFC Bank are highly liquid because they are frequently traded.

Volatility

  1. Definition: Volatility is a measure of the price fluctuations of a security or market index over a certain period. High volatility means prices can change dramatically in a short time.

  2. Example: The stock of a new technology startup might show high volatility due to speculative trading.

Price to Earnings (P/E) Ratio

  1. Definition: The P/E ratio is a valuation metric that compares a company’s current share price to its per-share earnings. It helps investors determine if a stock is overvalued or undervalued.

  2. Example: If a company's share price is ₹100 and its earnings per share (EPS) is ₹10, the P/E ratio is 10.

Bid-Ask Spread

  1. Definition: The bid-ask spread is the difference between the highest price a buyer is willing to pay for an asset (bid) and the lowest price a seller is willing to accept (ask).

  2. Example: If the bid price of a stock is ₹250 and the ask price is ₹255, the bid-ask spread is ₹5.

Blue-Chip Stocks

  1. Definition: Blue-chip stocks are shares of large, well-established, and financially sound companies with a history of reliable performance.

  2. Example: Infosys, TCS, and Reliance Industries are considered blue-chip stocks in India.

Index

  1. Definition: An index is a statistical measure that represents the performance of a group of stocks. It serves as a benchmark to gauge market performance.

  2. Example: The Nifty 50 is an index representing the performance of the top 50 companies listed on the NSE.

Capital Gains

  1. Definition: Capital gains are the profits earned from the sale of an asset, such as stocks, bonds, or real estate, at a price higher than the purchase price.

  2. Example: If you buy shares of Tata Motors at ₹200 and sell them at ₹250, the capital gain is ₹50 per share.

Earnings Per Share (EPS)

  1. Definition: EPS is a company's net profit divided by the number of outstanding shares. It indicates how much money a company makes per share of its stock.

  2. Example: If a company's net profit is ₹1 crore and it has 10 lakh outstanding shares, its EPS is ₹10.

Volume

  1. Definition: Volume refers to the number of shares traded in a security or market over a specific period. It indicates the level of activity and liquidity.

  2. Example: High trading volume in Reliance Industries shares indicates strong investor interest.

Margin Trading

  1. Definition: Margin trading involves borrowing money from a broker to purchase securities, allowing investors to buy more shares than they could with their available funds.

  2. Example: If you have ₹50,000 and your broker offers a 2:1 margin, you can buy ₹100,000 worth of stocks.

Short Selling

  1. Definition: Short selling is the practice of selling borrowed shares with the expectation that their price will decline, allowing the seller to buy them back at a lower price and profit from the difference.

  2. Example: If you short sell shares of Infosys at ₹1,500 and buy them back at ₹1,400, you make a profit of ₹100 per share.

Conclusion

Understanding key stock market concepts is essential for both novice and experienced investors. Each term reflects a fundamental aspect of market operations and investment strategies
