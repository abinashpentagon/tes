Types of Market

By Admin | Posted on 27 JULY 2024 | 07 min read

Markets include Primary Market, Secondary Market, Pre Market, Post Market

Post Market

The post-market session, also known as the after-hours trading session, refers to the period after the regular market hours during which investors can continue to trade securities. This session provides additional time for trading and can be influenced by news and events that occur after the market closes.

Key Features of the Post-Market Session

  1. Timing: In India, the post-market session occurs from 3:40 PM to 4:00 PM IST. This session is often referred to as the "closing session" and includes an additional period called the "post-closing session" from 4:00 PM to 4:30 PM IST.

    1. Closing Session (3:40 PM to 4:00 PM): This is primarily for order matching and finalizing trades at the closing price determined during the regular trading hours.

    2. Post-Closing Session (4:00 PM to 4:30 PM): Investors can place orders at the closing price for trades to be executed.

  2. Purpose: The post-market session allows investors to react to news and events that occur after the market closes. It also helps in order reconciliation and trade settlement.

  3. Order Types: In the post-market session, orders are typically placed at the closing price determined during regular trading hours. This helps in maintaining consistency and avoiding drastic price movements.

  4. Volatility and liquidity: Similar to the pre-market session, the post-market session often experiences lower liquidity and higher volatility compared to regular trading hours.

  5. Market Participants: Institutional investors, traders, and individual investors participate in the post-market session to manage their positions, react to late-breaking news, or take advantage of trading opportunities.

Benefits of Post-Market Trading

  1. Extended Trading Hours: Provides investors with more time to trade, especially if significant news or events occur after the regular market hours.

  2. Order Management: Allows investors to place, modify, or cancel orders based on the closing price, providing flexibility in managing their trades.

  3. Reacting to News: Enables investors to react to earnings reports, economic data releases, or other news that may be announced after the market closes.

Risks of Post-Market Trading

  1. Lower Liquidity: The post-market session usually has lower trading volumes, leading to wider bid-ask spreads and potentially less favorable prices.

  2. Higher Volatility: Prices can be more volatile due to fewer participants and lower liquidity, resulting in larger price swings.

  3. Limited Order Types: Certain order types, such as market orders, may not be accepted, limiting the strategies investors can employ.

Example of Pre-Market Session

Suppose a company announces an important merger deal after the market closes. Investors can place orders during the post-market session to react to this news. If the news is positive, the stock price might rise significantly in the post-market session.

Conclusion

The post-market session is an important part of the trading day, providing investors with additional time to trade securities and react to news and events that occur after regular market hours. While it offers benefits like extended trading hours and the ability to manage orders, it also comes with risks such as lower liquidity and higher volatility. Investors should be aware of these factors and stay informed to make the most of post-market trading.
